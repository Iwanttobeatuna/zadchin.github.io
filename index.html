About

I am a rising junior at Harvard concentrating in Computer Science and Mathematics. My interests include

  • Machine Learning; Particularly in healthcare
  • AI ethics, fairness and interpretability
  • NFTs and DAOs in their ability for social goods
  • Women in STEM from developing countries
  • Education Equity

Currently, I’m
  • Researching about underlying structure of MIMIC-III data, advised by Professor Finale Doshi-Velez in Harvard Data to Actionable Knowledge lab; Planning to publish the paper for MLHC conference by next Fall.
  • Working as a machine learning intern at DAOHQ
  • Writing guides on intuitively understanding AI and ML
Previously, I’ve
  • Taught CS courses at Harvard: Namely, I was a head teaching fellow for CS20(Discrete Mathematics for Computer Science) and course assistant for CS50(Introduction to Computer Science)
  • Worked as a software engineer at Harvard Tech 4 Social Good. Notably, I built a website wrapper for Department of Labor and NLP model for the CDC.
  • Founded Project BAWE(Beating Automation With Education), a Malaysia registered NPO that serves over 500 underprivileged children.
  • Founded Base Initiative, a project that helps underprivileged and underrepresented high schoolers in Malaysia to get scholarship.
  • Advocated for education equality in Malaysia in TEDx and UNDP (United Nation Development Programme) Malaysia

I'm always interested in meeting new people and hearing about potential collaborations. If you'd like to work together or get in contact with me, please email me at zadchin-at-college-dot-harvard-dot-edu or connect with me on LinkedIn.
